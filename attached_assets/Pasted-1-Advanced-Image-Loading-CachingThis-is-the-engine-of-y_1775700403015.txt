1. Advanced Image Loading & CachingThis is the "engine" of your viewer. Without this, the app will lag.Memory Caching: Storing recently viewed images in RAM so they reopen instantly.Disk Caching: Saving compressed versions (thumbnails) on the storage so the gallery doesn't have to re-decode high-res files every time you scroll.Asynchronous Loading: Loading images on a background thread so the UI doesn't freeze while a heavy 4K photo is being processed.2. The "Fluid" Gesture EngineThis is what makes the app feel "smooth" to the touch:Pinch-to-Zoom (Multi-touch): Using the distance between two fingers to scale the image.Fling/Inertia Scrolling: When you swipe fast, the image should keep moving slightly and slow down gradually, rather than stopping abruptly.Double-Tap to Toggle: A quick way to jump between "Fit to Screen" and "100% Zoom."Overscroll Elasticity: That "rubber-band" effect when you reach the edge of an image or the end of a folder.3. Metadata & EXIF HandlingA pro viewer doesn't just show pixels; it shows information.EXIF Parser: Extracting data like the camera model, ISO, focal length, and timestamp.Geotagging: Reading GPS coordinates from the file to show where the photo was taken (often integrated with a Map API).4. Optimization TechniquesFeatureImportanceWhy people love itBitmap Sub-samplingCriticalLoading a 20MB image at full resolution into RAM will crash a phone. You must load a version scaled to the screen size.Deep Zoom LogicHighFor very large images (like maps), the app should only load the specific "tile" or part of the image you are zoomed into.Pre-fetchingMediumAnticipating that the user will swipe right and loading the next image 20% in advance.5. Essential UI ComponentsImmersive Mode: Hiding the Status Bar and Navigation Bar so the photo takes up 100% of the screen.Shared Element Transitions: When you click a thumbnail, it should "grow" into the full-screen photo rather than just appearing instantly. This visual continuity is a hallmark of Google Photos.Thumbnail Grid: A fast-scrolling grid that uses "Lazy Loading" (only rendering what is visible on screen).
